Level B2 : Upper Intermediate

Goal: Understand the main ideas of complex text, interact fluently and spontaneously, and produce clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects.

Grammar

Review of All Tenses

At this level, you should master the use of all the tenses, including:

  • Present Tenses: Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous.
  • Past Tenses: Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous.
  • Future Tenses: Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous.

Examples:

  • I have been working here for two years. (Present Perfect Continuous)
  • She had already eaten when he arrived. (Past Perfect)
  • I will have finished the project by next week. (Future Perfect)

Mixed Conditionals

Definition: Used to describe hypothetical situations where the time in the "if" clause is different from the time in the main clause.

Structure:

  • If + past perfect, ... would + base verb (present result of a past condition).
  • If + past simple, ... would have + past participle (past result of a present condition).

Examples:

  • If I had studied harder, I would have a better job now.
  • If I were rich, I would have traveled the world last year.

Passive Voice (All Tenses)

Definition: The passive voice can now be used in all tenses.

Examples:

  • The house is being renovated. (Present Continuous)
  • The cake had been eaten by the time we arrived. (Past Perfect)
  • The documents will have been signed by tomorrow. (Future Perfect)

Reported Speech (Advanced)

Definition: Used to report what someone said, with more complex forms.

Examples:

  • Direct: He said, "I will call you tomorrow."
  • Reported: He said that he would call me the next day.

Changes in Time Expressions:

  • Today → that day.
  • Tomorrow → the next day.
  • Yesterday → the day before.

Relative Clauses (Defining and Non-Defining)

Definition:

  • Defining: Provides essential information about a noun.
    • The man who lives next door is my friend.
  • Non-Defining: Adds extra information (use commas).
    • My brother, who lives in Canada, is visiting us.

Modals of Deduction and Speculation

Definition: Used to express certainty, probability, or possibility.

Examples:

  • He must be at home. (certainty)
  • She might be working late. (possibility)
  • That can’t be true. (impossibility)

Causative (Have/Get Something Done)

Definition: Used when someone does something for you.

Structure: Subject + have/get + object + past participle.

Examples:

  • I had my car repaired yesterday.
  • She gets her hair cut every month.

Inversion with Negative Adverbials

Definition: Used for emphasis, especially in formal writing and speaking.

Examples:

  • Never have I seen such a beautiful place.
  • Not only did he apologize, but he also offered to help.

Participle Clauses

Definition: Used to add information, often replacing relative clauses.

Examples:

  • The man sitting by the window is my uncle. (Present participle)
  • Shocked by the news, she started crying. (Past participle)

Ellipsis and Substitution

Definition: Used to avoid repetition in sentences.

Examples:

  • She likes pizza, and I do too. (Substitution)
  • I went to the park, and John to the mall. (Ellipsis)

Vocabulary

Phrasal Verbs

Definition: Verbs combined with a preposition or adverb.

Examples:

  • Give up (quit): He gave up smoking.
  • Take off (remove): She took off her jacket.

Idiomatic Expressions

Definition: Phrases whose meanings are not literal.

Examples:

  • Break the ice: Start a conversation in a social situation.
  • Hit the nail on the head: Say something exactly right.

Collocations

Definition: Words that are commonly used together.

Examples:

  • Make a decision (not do a decision).
  • Strong coffee (not powerful coffee).

Formal and Informal Language

Examples:

  • Informal: Hi, can you help me?
  • Formal: Good afternoon, could you assist me?

Describing Abstract Topics

Examples:

  • Global warming, mental health, artificial intelligence.

Listening, Speaking, Reading, and Writing

Listening

Tasks:

  • Understand detailed conversations and arguments.
  • Identify speaker opinions, intentions, and attitudes.

Speaking

Tasks:

  • Participate fluently in discussions about abstract topics.
  • Explain viewpoints and give advantages and disadvantages of options.

Reading

Tasks:

  • Read complex articles and reports.
  • Identify main ideas, supporting details, and implicit meanings.

Writing

Tasks:

  • Write essays, reports, and reviews on a range of topics.
  • Use complex sentence structures and advanced vocabulary accurately.
