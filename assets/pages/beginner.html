Level A1 : Beginner

Goal : Understand and use basic everyday expressions and simple phrases.

Grammar

Let's begin with basics of grammar for A1.

The Alphabet & Pronunciation

Defintion : The English alphabet consists of 26 letters (A-Z). Learning the sounds of each letter is important for pronunciation.

Example :

-Vowels : A, E, I, O, U.

Consonants : B, C, D,F, G, etc.

Phonetic Example : "A" sounds like /eI/ (as in "apple"), "B" sounds like /bi:/ (as in banana).

Subject Pronouns

Definition : Subject pronouns are words that replace the subject of a sentence (who or what is doing the action).

List : I, you, he, she, it, we, they.

Examples :

  • I am happy
  • You are my friend.
  • He is a teacher.
  • They are students.

Verb "to be" (am, is, are)

Definition : The verb "to be" is used to describe a state or condition.

Forms :

  • I am
  • You/We/They are
  • He/She/It is
Examples :
  • I am a student.
  • She is a doctor.
  • They are friends.

Articles (a, an, the)

Definition : Articles are used before nouns to indicate whether the noun is specific or general.

Examples :
  • A / An : Used with singular, general nouns.
  • a book, an apple (use "an" before vowels: a, e, i, o, u).
  • The: Used with specific nouns.
  • The sun, The car.

Nouns

Definition : A noun is a word that represents a person, place, thing, or idea.

Singular/Plural:

Regular: dog/dogs

Irregular: man/men, child/children.

Examples :

  • This is a car. (singular)
  • These are cars. (plural)

Possesive Adjectives

Definition : Words that show ownership or possesion.

List : My, your, his, its, our, their.

Examples :

  • This is my book.
  • That is their house.

Demonstrative Pronouns

Definition : : Words that point to specific things.

List : This, that, these, those.

Examples :

  • This is my pen. (near, singular)
  • That is your bag. (far, singular)
  • These are my shoes. (near, plural)
  • Those are their cars. (far, plural)

Present Simple Tense

Definition : Used for habits, routines, and facts.

Structure :

Affirmative : Subject + Verb (base form) + Object.

  • I eat breakfast.

Negative : Subject + Do/Does not + Verb (base form).

  • He does not like tea.

Question : Do/Does + Subject + Verb (base form)?

  • Do you play football?

Examples :

  • She works in a school.
  • We don't watch TV.

Basic Prepositions of Place

Definition : Words used to show the location of something.

Examples :

  • The book is on the table.
  • The cat is under the chair.

Basic Prepositions of Time

Definition : Words used to show when something happens.

Examples :

  • We meet at 5 PM.
  • She was born in May.
  • They go to church on Sunday.

There is / There are

Definition : Used to indicate the presence of something.

Examples :

  • There is a book on the table. (singular)
  • There are two chairs in the room. (plural)

Can for Ability

Definition : Used to talk about what someone is able to do.

Examples :

  • I can swim
  • She can play the piano.

Imperatives

Definitions : Commands or instructions.

Examples :

  • Sit down.
  • Open your book.

Basic Question Words

Definition : Words used to ask questions.

List : What, where, when, who, why, how.

Examples :

  • What is your name?
  • Where do you live?
