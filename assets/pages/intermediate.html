Level B1 : Intermediate

Goal : Understand the main points on familiar matters. Deal with most travel situations, and produce simple connected text.

Grammar

Present Perfect Tense

Definition : Used for experiences or actions that happened at an unspecified time in the past, or actions that started in the past and continue in the present.

Structure : Subject + have/has + past participle.

Examples :

  • I have visited Paris. (experience)
  • She has just finished her homework. (recent action)
  • They have lived here for five years.* (action continuing to present)

Keywords : For, since, already, yet, just, ever, never.

  • I have never been to Japan.
  • Have you ever tried sushi?

Past Continuous Tense

Definition : Used for actions that were in progress at a specific time in the past.

Structure : Subject + was/were + verb (-ing).

Examples :

  • I was reading a book when the phone rang.
  • They were playing football at 6 PM yesterday.

Negative : I wasn't sleeping.

Questions : Were you watching TV?

Future Simple (Will)

Definition : Used for spontaneous decisions, promises, or predictions.

Structure : Subject + will + base verb.

Examples :

  • I will help you with your homework.* (promise)
  • She will call you tomorrow.* (prediction)

Future Continuous

Definition : Used for actions that will be in progress at a specific time in the future.

Structure : Subject + will be + verb (-ing).

Examples :

  • This time tomorrow, I will be flying to New York.
  • They will be studying at 8 PM.

Comparatives and Superlatives (Advanced)

Adding complexity to comparisons:

As...as : Used to compare two things equally.

  • She is as tall as her brother.

Less...than : Used to show a lower degree.

  • This book is less interesting than the other one.

Modals of Obligation, Advice, and Possibility

Must/Have to : Obligation.

  • You must wear a seatbelt.
  • I have to finish this report.

Should : Advice

  • You should eat more vegetables.

Might/May/Could : Possibility

  • It might rain tommorow.

First Conditional

Definition : Used for real future possibilities.

Structure : If + present simple, ... will + base verb.

Examples :

  • If it rains, we will stay inside.
  • She will pass the exam if she studies hard.

Second Conditional

Definition : Used for hypothetical or unlikely situations.

Structure : If + past simple, ... would + base verb.

Examples :

  • If i had a car, I would drive to work.
  • She would travel the world if she won the lottery.

Passive Voice

Definition : Used when the focus is on the action, not the doer.

Structure : Subject + be (in correct tense) + past participle.

Examples :

  • The cake was baked by Sarah.* (past simple)
  • The homework is being done.* (present continuous)

Reported Speech

Definition : Used to report what someone said, without quoting them directly.

Examples :

    Direct : She said, "I am tired."

    Reported : She said that she was tired.

Relative Clauses

Definition : Used to give more information about noun.

Examples :

  • This is the book that I was reading.
  • The man who lives next door is a doctor.

Gerunds and Infinitives

Definition : Verbs that can act as nouns.

Examples :

  • I enjoy swimming. (gerund)
  • I want to learn English. (infinitive)

Used to for Past Habits

Definition : Used to describe actions or states in the past that no longer happen.

Examples :

  • I used to play football every weekend.
  • She didn't use to like coffee.

Listening, Speaking, Reading, and Writing

Listening:

  • Practice understanding the main points of conversations or short talks.
  • Example: Listen to a dialogue about booking a hotel.

Speaking:

  • Start participating in discussions about familiar topics.
  • Example: Talk about your last vacation.

Reading:

  • Read simple articles, blog posts, or stories.
  • Example: Read about a tourist destination.

Writing:

  • Write paragraphs describing experiences or giving opinions.
  • Example: Write about your favorite hobby.
