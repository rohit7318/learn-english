Level C2 : Proficiency

Goal: Understand with ease virtually everything heard or read. Summarize information from different spoken and written sources, reconstructing arguments and accounts coherently. Express yourself spontaneously, very fluently, and precisely, differentiating finer shades of meaning even in more complex situations.

Grammar

Mastery of All Grammar Structures

At this level, all grammar concepts should be second nature. The focus is on accuracy, appropriateness, and style.

Subtle Nuances in Modal Verbs

Definition: Use modal verbs for expressing subtle differences in certainty, obligation, or possibility.

Examples:

  • He might have been working late. (speculating about the past)
  • You could have told me earlier. (expressing missed opportunity)

Advanced Passive Voice

Definition: Use advanced passive forms for formal or academic language.

Examples:

  • The study is said to have been conducted in 2022. (formal passive)
  • It is thought that the project will be completed soon.

Inversion for Emphasis

Definition: Use inversion to create emphasis in formal contexts.

Examples:

  • Had I known about the meeting, I would have attended. (instead of If I had known...)
  • Little did she realize how important her role was.

Advanced Relative Clauses

Definition: Use relative clauses to add complexity to sentences.

Examples:

  • The book, the contents of which were fascinating, was a bestseller.
  • This is the reason why I decided to leave.

Nominalization for Formal Writing

Definition: Use nominalization to make writing sound more formal and academic.

Examples:

  • Decide → decision: The decision to expand the business was unanimous.
  • Improve → improvement: There has been significant improvement in her grades.

Conditionals with Nuance

Definition: Use mixed and implied conditionals to express hypothetical or nuanced situations.

Examples:

  • If I hadn’t been so tired, I might have agreed to join you. (past condition, past result)
  • Were it not for his help, we wouldn’t have succeeded. (formal inversion)

Ellipsis and Cohesion

Definition: Use ellipsis for conciseness and cohesion.

Examples:

  • She went to the park, and I to the mall. (ellipsis)
  • I can play the piano, and so can he. (substitution)

Vocabulary

Advanced Collocations

Definition: Use natural combinations of words to sound fluent and precise.

Examples:

  • Make a compelling argument.
  • Provide a detailed analysis.

Idiomatic and Figurative Language

Definition: Master idioms, expressions, and metaphors to sound natural and fluent.

Examples:

  • Throw in the towel: Admit defeat.
  • The ball is in your court: It’s your decision now.

Formal and Academic Vocabulary

Definition: Use precise, formal language for academic or professional contexts.

Examples:

  • Mitigate, exacerbate, facilitate, substantiate, elucidate.

Nuanced Synonyms

Definition: Use synonyms with subtle differences in meaning to convey precise ideas.

Examples:

  • Big → colossal, immense, substantial.
  • Important → crucial, pivotal, significant.

Specialized Vocabulary

Definition: Learn vocabulary specific to your field of interest (e.g., medicine, technology, law).

Examples:

  • Algorithm, litigation, hypothesis, metabolism.

Listening, Speaking, Reading, and Writing

Listening

Focus: Understanding implicit meanings, satire, irony, and culturally specific references.

Tasks:

  • Listen to academic lectures, debates, and interviews.
  • Identify tone, attitude, and speaker’s intent.

Speaking

Focus: Spontaneous, nuanced, and context-appropriate speech.

Tasks:

  • Participate in debates and discussions on abstract and complex topics.
  • Use idioms and metaphors naturally.
  • Speak with near-native fluency and precision.

Reading

Focus: Deep comprehension of complex and abstract texts.

Tasks:

  • Read academic journals, research papers, and novels.
  • Analyze author intent, tone, and bias.
  • Summarize and critique ideas effectively.

Writing

Focus: Producing clear, coherent, and stylistically appropriate texts.

Tasks:

  • Write essays, reports, and critiques with advanced structure and vocabulary.
  • Use cohesive devices and advanced grammar for clarity.
  • Example: Moreover, consequently, however, notwithstanding, in addition.
