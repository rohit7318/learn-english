Level A2 : Elementary

Goal : Understand sentences and frequently used expressions on familiar topics. Communicate in simple, routine tasks.

Grammar

Review A1 Grammar

Before moving forward, make sure you're comfortable with A1 level grammar(e.g., present simple tense, prepositions of time/place, articles). If you need a fresher go back again to A1 Level Grammar.

Present Continuous Tense

Definition : Used to describe actions happening right now or around the present moment.

Structure : Subject + am/is/are + Verb (-ing form).

Examples :

  • I am eating lunch. (action happening now)
  • She is studying English. (action happening around now)
  • Negative : I am not sleeping. / He isn't working.
  • Questions : Are you listening? / What is she doing?

Past Simple Tense

Definition : Used to talk about completed actions in the past.

Regular Verbs : Add -ed to the base verb.

  • I visited my friend yesterday.
  • She walked to school.

Irregular Verbs : Learn their unique past forms.

  • I went to the park. (go => went)
  • They ate pizza (eat => ate)

Negative : Use did not (didn't) + base verb.

  • I did not watch TV.

Questions : Use Did + Subject + base verb.

  • Did you visit the museum?

Was / Were (Past Simple of "to be")

Definition : The past form of am/is/are.

Examples :

  • I was happy.
  • Negative : I wasn't tired.
  • Questions : Were you at home?

Countable and Uncountable Nouns

Definitions : Nouns can be countable (things you can count) or uncountable (things you can't count)

Examples :

  • Countable : apple/apples, chair/chairs
  • Uncountable : water, rice , sugar

Quantifiers :

  • Some : I have some apples. / I need some water.
  • Any : Do you have any apples? / I don't have any rice.

Object Pronouns :

Definition : Pronouns that replace the object of a sentence.

List : Me, you, him, her, it, us, them.

Examples :

  • I saw her at the park.
  • They gave it to me.

Possesive Pronouns

Definition : Words that show ownership.

List : Mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs.

Examples :

  • This book is mine.
  • That pen is hers.

Comparative Adjectives

Definition : Used to compare two things.

Structure : Add -er to short adjectives or use more with longer ones.

  • This book is bigger than that one.
  • She is more beautiful than her sister.

Superlative Adjectives

Definition : Used to compare three or more things to show the highest degree.

Structure : Add -est to short adjectives or use most with longer ones.

  • He is the tallest boy in the class.
  • This is the most interesting book.

Going to Future

Defnition : Used for plans or intentions.

Structure : Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb.

Examples :

  • I am going to watch a movie tonight.
  • She is going to visit her grandparents.

Adverbs of Frequency

Definition : Words that describe how often something happens.

Examples : Always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never.

Sentences :

  • I always wake up early.
  • They sometimes go hiking.

Prepositions of Movement

Definition : Words that describe movement from one place to another.

Examples :

  • He ran into the room.
  • She walked out of the house.

Conjuctions

Definition : Words that connect two parts of a sentence.

Examples :

  • And I like apples and mangoes.
  • But She is smart but lazy.
  • Because : He stayed home because he was sick.
  • So : It was raining, so we stayed inside.

Can/Can't for Permission Ability

Examples :

  • You can go now. (permission)
  • I can't Swim. (ability)

Have to / Don't Have to

Definition : Used to describe necessity.

Examples :

  • I have to finish my homework.
  • You don't have to come if you don't want to.

Question Forms

Examples :

  • Past : where did you go yesterday?
  • Present : What are you doing now?

Listening, Speaking, Reading, and Writing

Listening

  • Focus on slightly longer dialogues.
  • Practice recognizing key details (e.g., numbers, locations, times)

Speaking

  • Use daily routines and past events for practice.
  • Example : What did you do yesterday?

Reading

  • Read A2 level graded readers.
  • Example : Simple stories or articles.

Writing

  • Practice writing short paragraphs about your routines or past events.

    • Example : Write about *What you did last weekend.
